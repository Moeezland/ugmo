About UGMO

A multilateral intermicronational alliance focused on collective defense, political coordination, and trade facilitation among sovereign micronations. The organization operates under a charter-based system emphasizing mutual non-aggression, collective security, democratic decision-making, and regulated economic cooperation. Membership is subject to approval and compliance with neutrality and conflict-related restrictions. UGMO maintains observer and full membership statuses and elects its leadership on a rotational basis.

Structure of UGMO

Security Alliance

Members cannot attack each other. If a member is attacked, all members are obligated to defend them. This applies only to defensive wars, not aggression

Governance

Members are admitted via approval or voting. Observer states exist but cannot vote. No single member has superior authority. Leadership is rotational (every 6 months)

Membership Standards

States actively at war or with extreme negative relations cannot join. Aggressors are excluded. New members must accept the charter.

Economic & trade cooperation

UGMO supports inter-member trade. Trade is encouraged but not compulsory between all members. Tariffs/sanctions are restricted and regulated. Bilateral trade agreements are allowed.

Rule-making

Charter is the highest authority. New rules are introduced as bills. Major decisions use majority voting (50.1%), charter changes require full approval

Security Council

Handles security and defense matters within UGMO. Oversees conflicts, sanctions, and charter violations. Acts as a rapid-response body, operating within the charter and member oversight.
